The history of conflict between Pakistan and India is rooted in the partition of British India in 1947, which led to the creation of the two nations and the immediate dispute over the princely state of Kashmir. Since then, the two countries have fought multiple wars—most notably in 1947–48, 1965, 1971, and the Kargil conflict in 1999—each arising from territorial tensions, particularly around Kashmir. These wars have not only cost thousands of lives but have also left deep political and emotional scars, leading to one of the most enduring rivalries in South Asia. While periods of peace and dialogue have occasionally emerged, mutual distrust, border skirmishes, and nuclear capabilities continue to define their relationship, making it a flashpoint in global geopolitics.
